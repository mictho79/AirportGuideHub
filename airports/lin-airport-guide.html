Milan Linate Airport Guide (LIN) – Milan

IATA: LIN ICAO: LIML Milan, Italy 1 Terminal

Airport Overview

Milan Linate Airport (LIN) is the closest airport to Milan's city center, situated just 7 km east of the city in the Segrate municipality. Though smaller than Malpensa, Linate handles approximately 9 million passengers annually and is favored by business travelers for its exceptional proximity and efficiency. After a major renovation completed in 2020, the airport was completely transformed into a modern, light-filled terminal with premium retail, world-class dining, and significantly improved passenger flow. Linate primarily serves European routes, functioning as a feeder for ITA Airways (formerly Alitalia) and connecting Milan's business community to major European hubs including London, Paris, Frankfurt, and Amsterdam.

Terminals

Milan Linate operates from a single, modern terminal building:

  • Main Terminal: The fully renovated terminal building completed in 2020, featuring a bright, airy design with high-end retail brands (including Armani, Gucci, and Bottega Veneta outlets), premium dining, and state-of-the-art security and boarding facilities.
  • Schengen Gates (A/B): For European Schengen flights, accommodating ITA Airways domestic and European routes, British Airways, Air France, Lufthansa, and other Schengen-area carriers.
  • Non-Schengen Gates (C): For international flights outside the Schengen area including UK-bound flights post-Brexit.
💡 Linate underwent a complete 3-year renovation and reopened in September 2020 with an entirely new layout. The airport is now one of Italy's most architecturally impressive, inspired by Milanese design values and featuring works by Italian artists throughout the terminal.

WiFi Availability

Free WiFi is available throughout Milan Linate Airport under the network "SEA WiFi" (shared with Malpensa, operated by SEA Milan Airports). Connect and register with an email address for unlimited free access. The service provides fast, reliable connectivity at 20–40 Mbps throughout the renovated terminal. The new terminal design includes dedicated charging stations and high-speed WiFi hubs at every gate area for business travelers.

Transport to City Center

Metro Line 4 (M4 – Blue Line)

The new Milan Metro Line 4 (M4) opened in 2023 and provides a direct underground link from Linate Airport to Milan city center. The journey to Duomo (city center) takes approximately 12 minutes, with stops at Forlanini, Susa, Argonne, Dateo, S. Babila, and Duomo. Trains run every 3–5 minutes during peak hours. The fare is €1.50 on an ATM Urbano ticket. This is the fastest and most convenient transport option available and a major improvement over previous bus-only options.

Bus Services

ATM Bus Line 73 continues to run from the airport to San Babila (city center) in approximately 25 minutes, with a standard city transport fare. This is an alternative for passengers who prefer the surface route or are traveling with heavy luggage. Bus 73 runs throughout the day from approximately 05:30 to 00:30.

Taxi and Rideshare

White Milanese taxis queue outside arrivals. The fixed fare from Linate to Milan city center (within the city municipality) is approximately €10–15. The journey takes 15–25 minutes depending on traffic. Uber and FREE NOW operate at Linate with competitive pricing.

Airport Lounges

  • ITA Airways Lounge (Schengen, Airside): A premium lounge for ITA Airways Business Class passengers and Volare elite members, featuring Italian aperitivo-style catering, espresso bar, and comfortable seating with runway views.
  • Sala Monteverdi (Priority Pass Lounge): An independent lounge accessible with Priority Pass and day-pass purchase, featuring a full Italian buffet, cocktail bar, and business workstations.
  • British Airways/oneworld Lounge: For BA Business Class and oneworld Emerald members on BA-operated flights, with premium wines and British and Italian-inspired snacks.
  • Air France/KLM Lounge: For SkyTeam Business Class passengers on Air France and KLM flights, with a la carte service and complimentary drinks.
  • Aspire Lounge (Non-Schengen): For Priority Pass holders on UK and non-Schengen departures, with light refreshments and quiet seating.

Hotels Near the Airport

  • Marriott Milan Linate: Located 5 minutes from the terminal with complimentary shuttle, featuring contemporary rooms, a fitness center, and a restaurant serving Lombard cuisine.
  • Hotel MiCo (Segrate): A business hotel in the Segrate district 2 km from the airport, near the Milan East business park and popular with corporate travelers.
  • AC Hotel Bella Sky (Linate Area): A stylish 4-star hotel 3 km from the airport ideal for fashion and design industry professionals.
  • Ibis Milano Est: A reliable budget option in the eastern Milan suburbs, 4 km from Linate, with ATM public transport stops nearby.
  • Hilton Milan: For those preferring a central Milan location, the Hilton near the Duomo is reachable in 12 minutes via the new M4 metro line from Linate.

Food and Restaurants

  • Terrazza Triennale (Airside): A landmark restaurant collaboration inspired by Milan's famous Triennale design museum, serving Lombard risotto, ossobuco, and Milanese classics in an art-gallery setting.
  • Carlo e Camilla (Airside): A Michelin-starred chef's airport concept offering modern Italian cuisine, innovative cocktails, and a curated wine cellar.
  • Caffe Vergnano 1882: The historic Turin coffee brand with multiple locations throughout the terminal serving exceptional espresso, cornetti (Italian croissants), and aperitivos.
  • Obika Mozzarella Bar: Fresh buffalo mozzarella, burrata, and Italian antipasti — an authentic southern Italian experience in the departures area.
  • Armani Caffe: A café concept by the Armani fashion house, serving light Italian fare, Aperol Spritz, and Milanese aperitivo in a sleek designer setting.
  • Pret a Manger: Available for quick grab-and-go meals with fresh sandwiches, salads, and hot drinks.

Travel Tips

  • The new Metro Line 4 (M4) from Linate to Duomo in 12 minutes is a game-changer — it's the fastest, cheapest, and most stress-free way to reach Milan's city center.
  • Linate is significantly more convenient than Malpensa for business travelers staying in central Milan — check whether your flight operates from LIN or MXP before booking accommodation.
  • The airport's luxury retail zone is genuinely impressive post-renovation, with authentic Italian fashion brands at competitive duty-free prices — allow time if shopping is planned.
  • The aperitivo culture is strong even at the airport — many bars and cafés serve complimentary snacks with drinks from 18:00 onwards, reflecting Milanese tradition.
  • Traffic on the A1 motorway east of Milan can cause significant delays — the M4 metro eliminates this risk entirely for city center travelers.
  • Linate has a cap on operations (no night flights, limited total movements) that keeps it less congested than Malpensa — a major advantage for business travelers.
  • Milan is Europe's fashion capital — even the airport's bathrooms feature Milanese design details, reflecting the city's obsession with aesthetics.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far is Milan Linate Airport from the city center?

Milan Linate Airport is just 7 km east of Milan's Duomo (city center), making it one of the closest airports to a European city center. With the new Metro Line 4 (M4), the journey from Linate to Duomo takes approximately 12 minutes and costs €1.50.

What is the difference between Milan Linate and Milan Malpensa airports?

Linate (LIN) is 7 km from central Milan and primarily handles European and domestic routes — it's significantly more convenient for city-center destinations. Malpensa (MXP) is 49 km from Milan and handles intercontinental flights and low-cost carrier European routes. Choose Linate when flying European airlines to/from central Milan for the fastest city transfer.

Is there a direct metro from Milan Linate to the Duomo?

Yes — the Metro Line 4 (M4, Blue Line) opened in 2023 and runs directly from Linate Airport to Duomo station in approximately 12 minutes. It is the fastest and cheapest way to reach Milan city center and a major improvement for business travelers who previously relied on bus services.

When did Milan Linate Airport reopen after renovation?

Milan Linate closed for a major 14-month renovation from July 2019 and reopened in September 2020. The renovation completely transformed the terminal with a new design inspired by Milanese architecture and design values, new retail including luxury Italian fashion brands, improved security lanes, and upgraded passenger facilities.
