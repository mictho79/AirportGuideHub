Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport Guide (MSP) – Minneapolis

IATA: MSP ICAO: KMSP Minneapolis, USA 2 Terminals

Airport Overview

Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport (MSP) is the primary commercial airport for the Twin Cities metropolitan area and the Upper Midwest, serving approximately 40 million passengers annually. Located 10 miles south of downtown Minneapolis near the historic Fort Snelling site, MSP serves as a major hub for Delta Air Lines and a significant focus city for Sun Country Airlines. The airport offers nonstop service to over 150 domestic and 30 international destinations, with Delta's extensive network making MSP one of the best-connected mid-sized cities in North America for direct flights.

MSP consistently receives high marks for cleanliness, customer service, and operational efficiency. The airport operates with two terminals — the main Lindbergh Terminal (Terminal 1) named after aviator Charles Lindbergh who was born in Minnesota, and the smaller Humphrey Terminal (Terminal 2) named after former Vice President Hubert Humphrey. The airport holds a LEED Gold certification for its environmental programs and is one of the most energy-efficient airports of its size in the United States.

Terminals

MSP operates two terminals: the Lindbergh Terminal (Terminal 1) and the Humphrey Terminal (Terminal 2), connected by a free tram service.

  • Terminal 1 (Lindbergh): Delta Air Lines hub operations (all concourses), American Airlines, United Airlines, Alaska Airlines, international carriers; Concourses C, D, E, F, and G
  • Terminal 2 (Humphrey): Sun Country Airlines, Spirit Airlines, Frontier Airlines, some charter operations; Concourses H and K
💡 Terminal 1 and Terminal 2 are connected by a free tram that runs every 5–8 minutes. The tram drops passengers at the ticketing level of each terminal; you must go through security separately at each terminal. Allow 45+ minutes for any cross-terminal connection.

WiFi Availability

MSP provides complimentary WiFi throughout both terminals via the "MSP Free WiFi" network. Connect by selecting the network and accepting the terms of service — no password, registration, or time limits required. Coverage is excellent throughout Terminal 1's extensive concourses and Terminal 2. Charging stations are widely available at gate areas throughout both terminals. Boingo Wireless premium plans are available for travelers needing faster dedicated bandwidth.

Transport to City Center

Metro Transit Blue Line (Light Rail)

The Metro Transit Blue Line light rail provides a direct connection between MSP and downtown Minneapolis. The Terminal 1-Lindbergh station is located adjacent to the Lindbergh Terminal's baggage claim level, accessible via a short covered walkway. The journey to downtown Minneapolis (Nicollet Mall or Government Plaza stations) takes approximately 25 minutes and costs $2.00 per trip ($2.50 during peak hours). Trains run every 7–10 minutes during peak times from approximately 5:00 AM to midnight daily. This is the most reliable and cost-effective option for reaching downtown Minneapolis.

Bus Services

Metro Transit bus routes serve both terminals with connections throughout the Twin Cities metro area. Express bus Route 54 connects MSP to downtown Minneapolis and Saint Paul. Various hotel shuttle services and Groome Transportation provide connections to suburban communities.

Taxi and Rideshare

Taxis and rideshare services (Uber, Lyft) pick up from the Ground Transportation Center at Terminal 1. A taxi to downtown Minneapolis costs approximately $30–$40 and takes 20–30 minutes. Rideshare fares run $20–$35. Highway 62 and I-35W can experience significant congestion during weekday rush hours; the light rail is often faster and more predictable.

Airport Lounges

MSP has solid lounge options anchored by Delta's Sky Club presence and growing independent alternatives.

  • Delta Sky Club – Concourse G: Delta's primary MSP lounge with hot food service, cocktail bar, and shower suites; accessible to SkyMiles Medallion members and Sky Club cardholders
  • Delta Sky Club – Concourse F: Second Delta Sky Club location serving Concourses E and F gate areas
  • American Airlines Admirals Club – Concourse G: For AAdvantage Executive Platinum members and Admirals Club cardholders
  • The Escape Lounge (Terminal 1, Concourse G): Priority Pass–accepting lounge with food, beverages, and a quiet atmosphere
  • United Club – Concourse F: For MileagePlus Premier members and United Club cardholders

Hotels Near the Airport

Multiple hotels are located adjacent to or within minutes of MSP, several connected directly to the terminal via walkways or the tram system.

  • InterContinental Minneapolis-Saint Paul Airport: Connected directly to Terminal 1 via a sky bridge — no shuttle needed; upscale full-service hotel with multiple dining venues
  • Crowne Plaza Minneapolis Airport: Directly across from Terminal 2 with complimentary shuttle; convenient for Sun Country passengers
  • Courtyard by Marriott Minneapolis Airport: 5 minutes from Terminal 1 with complimentary shuttle and full restaurant service
  • Hyatt Regency Bloomington: Located near Mall of America and MSP, accessible via light rail; full-service hotel with pool and spa
  • Embassy Suites Minneapolis Airport: All-suite hotel with complimentary breakfast, approximately 5 minutes from Terminal 1 via shuttle

Food and Restaurants

MSP has developed a strong airport dining program, with Minnesota-inspired concepts and local Twin Cities brands featured prominently in both terminals.

  • Ike's Food & Cocktails (Concourse G): Minneapolis cocktail lounge institution with craft cocktails and elevated bar food inspired by the Twin Cities food scene
  • Smashburger (Concourse C): Denver-born but widely popular chain, excellent for a quick pre-flight burger
  • French Meadow Bakery & Café (Concourse G): Minneapolis-based organic bakery and restaurant with farm-to-table items, craft coffee, and whole-grain breads
  • Grain Belt Premium Tap Room (Concourse F): Celebrating Minnesota's most iconic beer brand with a rotating craft tap list and classic American pub fare
  • Prairie Dogs Hot Dogs (Concourse D): Minnesota-style hotdogs with local and artisan toppings, a popular quick-service option
  • Starbucks (Multiple locations): Available in both Terminal 1 concourses and Terminal 2

Travel Tips

  • Arrive 2.5 hours before domestic flights and 3 hours before international — MSP security in Terminal 1 can be very long, particularly during Delta's morning departure banks.
  • Minnesota winters are serious: blizzards and ice storms can cause significant delays and cancellations from November through March. Monitor flight status and plan for flexibility during winter travel.
  • TSA PreCheck is available at both Terminal 1 and Terminal 2 security checkpoints; CLEAR is available at Terminal 1.
  • The Metro Blue Line is the most reliable option for downtown Minneapolis — don't underestimate how bad I-494 traffic can be during weekday rush hours.
  • MSP's Terminal 1 concourses are very long — the distance from Concourse C to Concourse G is substantial. Check your gate early and position yourself at the correct end of the terminal.
  • The Mall of America (the largest shopping mall in the U.S.) is just 10 minutes from the airport via light rail — easy for a layover excursion.
  • Long-term parking fills rapidly during holiday periods — reserve online in advance through MSP's official parking portal.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get from Minneapolis-Saint Paul Airport to downtown Minneapolis?

The Metro Transit Blue Line light rail is the best option — 25 minutes to downtown Minneapolis for $2.00–$2.50. The station is adjacent to Terminal 1 via a covered walkway. Taxis cost $30–$40 and rideshare runs $20–$35. Rush hour traffic on I-35W can extend road travel to 45+ minutes.

Is WiFi free at Minneapolis-Saint Paul Airport?

Yes, MSP offers free WiFi throughout both terminals via the "MSP Free WiFi" network. No password, registration, or time limits apply. Boingo Wireless premium plans offer faster speeds. Charging stations are plentiful at gate areas throughout both terminals.

How do I get from Terminal 1 to Terminal 2 at MSP?

A free tram connects Terminal 1 (Lindbergh) and Terminal 2 (Humphrey) and runs every 5–8 minutes. The tram drops passengers at the ticketing level of each terminal. Note that you must go through separate security at each terminal — there is no airside connection. Allow at least 45 minutes for any connection between terminals.

Does MSP have a Priority Pass lounge?

Yes — The Escape Lounge in Concourse G of Terminal 1 accepts Priority Pass memberships. It also offers day passes for non-members. Delta Sky Clubs do not accept Priority Pass but are accessible with SkyMiles Medallion status or a Sky Club membership card.
