Chengdu Tianfu International Airport Guide (TFU) – Chengdu

IATA: TFU ICAO: ZUUU Chengdu, China 2 Terminals

Airport Overview

Chengdu Tianfu International Airport (TFU) opened in June 2021 as a second major airport for Chengdu, capital of Sichuan Province, located approximately 50 km southeast of the city centre. The airport is designed to eventually handle 90 million passengers per year when fully expanded, making it one of the largest airports in China. Chengdu Shuangliu (CTU) continues to operate as Chengdu's first airport, and the two airports together form one of China's few dual-airport city systems. Sichuan Airlines and several other carriers have transferred key operations to TFU.

Terminals

  • Terminal T1: Handles domestic operations primarily for Sichuan Airlines, Chengdu Airlines, and other carriers transferred from Shuangliu.
  • Terminal T2: International terminal handling international routes, select domestic operations, and premium carriers. Features dedicated check-in zones and international lounges.
💡 Chengdu has two international airports: Tianfu (TFU) and Shuangliu (CTU). Verify which airport your flight uses before travelling — they are approximately 100 km apart. For TFU, allow ample time for the 50+ km journey from the city centre.

WiFi Availability

Free Wi-Fi is available at Chengdu Tianfu Airport under the network "TFU_Airport_WiFi". Registration with a Chinese mobile number is required through the landing portal. International travellers without a Chinese SIM should purchase one at the airport. As with all mainland China airports, a VPN installed before arrival is required to access Google, WhatsApp, and other Western services blocked by the Great Firewall.

Transport to City Centre

Chengdu Metro Line 18

Chengdu Metro Line 18 connects Tianfu Airport to central Chengdu. The line runs from TFU directly to Tianfu Square (city centre) in approximately 30–35 minutes. Fares are CNY 10–15. Trains operate from approximately 06:30 to 23:30 with peak-hour frequency of 5–8 minutes. This is the fastest and most affordable city-centre link.

Bus Services

Multiple airport express buses serve key Chengdu destinations including Chengdu East Railway Station, Shuangliu (the other airport), and major commercial districts. Journey times are 40–80 minutes. Fares are CNY 20–40.

Taxi and Rideshare

Taxis and DiDi are available from the designated zones outside T1 and T2. The journey to central Chengdu (Tianfu Square/Chunxi Road) takes approximately 50–70 minutes and costs CNY 120–180 including tolls.

Airport Lounges

TFU's new terminals feature well-appointed lounges reflecting the airport's premium positioning.

  • Sichuan Airlines Business Lounge (T1): Hot Chinese buffet with Sichuan-inspired dishes (mapo tofu, dan dan noodles), beverages, and shower rooms.
  • Air China International Lounge (T2): Star Alliance accessible premium lounge with international dining and shower facilities.
  • China Eastern Business Lounge (T1): Full hot buffet lounge for SkyTeam members and business class passengers.
  • International VIP Lounge (T2): Accepts Priority Pass and DragonPass; full dining, showers, and business services.
  • CIP Pay-Per-Use Lounge (T1 and T2): Available to all passengers; hot buffet, Wi-Fi, and comfortable seating.

Hotels Near the Airport

The Tianfu New Area surrounding TFU has several hotels, with more options in central Chengdu accessible by metro.

  • Wanda Vista Tianfu (on-site area): Luxury hotel within the Tianfu New Area, approximately 5 km from TFU, with rooftop pool and multiple dining venues.
  • Marriott Chengdu Tianfu: Approximately 8 km from TFU in the Tianfu district, full-service business hotel.
  • The St. Regis Chengdu: Five-star luxury hotel approximately 45 km from TFU in central Chengdu; worth the metro trip for a premium city stay.
  • Hampton Inn Chengdu Tianfu Airport: Budget option approximately 3 km from TFU with free shuttle service and breakfast included.
  • JW Marriott Hotel Chengdu: Flagship luxury hotel approximately 45 km away in the city centre near Chunxi Road shopping.

Food and Restaurants

Chengdu is globally renowned for its cuisine, and the airport does not disappoint — Sichuan flavours dominate the terminal dining scene.

  • Grandma's Kitchen (T1): Traditional Sichuan restaurant serving mapo tofu, kung pao chicken, and dan dan noodles — authentic and outstanding.
  • McDonald's (T1 and T2): Full menu with Sichuan-inspired McSpicy options and standard favourites.
  • Starbucks (T2): Standard menu with China-exclusive drinks near the international departure gates.
  • Sichuan Hot Pot Bar (T1): Mini hotpot station allowing passengers to sample the world-famous Chengdu mala hotpot experience before departure.
  • KFC (T1): Standard and China-exclusive menu items widely popular with domestic travellers.
  • Luckin Coffee (T1 and T2): Affordable specialty coffee chain; convenient and fast for early-morning departures.

Travel Tips

  • Chengdu is the gateway to the Sichuan Giant Panda Breeding Research Base — book a morning visit to see pandas during their active feeding time (08:00–10:00).
  • Chengdu has two airports: TFU (Tianfu) and CTU (Shuangliu). They are 100 km apart — always confirm which airport your flight uses to avoid missed departures.
  • Metro Line 18 is the single most important piece of travel advice for TFU visitors — fast, cheap, and connects directly to the city centre.
  • Install a VPN and DiDi before arriving in China; both are essential for navigation and transportation.
  • Chengdu's Sichuan cuisine is famously spicy; if you have a low heat tolerance, communicate this when ordering (ask for "bu yao la" — no spicy).
  • China's 144-hour visa-free transit policy applies to Chengdu for eligible nationalities.
  • WeChat Pay and Alipay are universally accepted in Chengdu — link a foreign bank card to Alipay before travel.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get from Chengdu Tianfu Airport to the city centre?

Metro Line 18 is the best option, taking 30–35 minutes from TFU to Tianfu Square for CNY 10–15. Taxis and DiDi cost CNY 120–180 and take 50–70 minutes. Airport express buses cost CNY 20–40 and take 40–80 minutes depending on destination.

What is the difference between Chengdu TFU and CTU airports?

TFU (Tianfu) opened in 2021 as Chengdu's second major airport, located 50 km southeast of the city. CTU (Shuangliu) is the original airport, 18 km from the city centre. Different airlines use different airports — always check your itinerary and boarding pass to confirm which airport you need.

Can I see pandas near Chengdu Tianfu Airport?

Yes — the Chengdu Giant Panda Breeding Research Base is approximately 65 km from TFU (accessible via Metro Line 18 + Line 3, about 70 minutes total). The morning feeding hours (08:00–10:00) are the best time to see pandas. Book tickets online in advance as they sell out on busy days.

Does Chengdu Tianfu Airport have Priority Pass lounges?

Yes, the International VIP Lounge in T2 accepts Priority Pass and DragonPass memberships. Pay-per-use CIP lounges are available in both T1 and T2 for all passengers. Airline lounges (Sichuan Airlines, Air China, China Eastern) require business class tickets or status membership.
