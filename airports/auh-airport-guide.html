Abu Dhabi International Airport Guide (AUH) – Abu Dhabi

IATA: AUH ICAO: OMAA Abu Dhabi, UAE 3 Terminals

Airport Overview

Abu Dhabi International Airport (AUH) is the main international gateway to the UAE capital, located approximately 30 km east of Abu Dhabi city centre. The airport serves as the primary hub for Etihad Airways, the UAE's national carrier, and handled over 23 million passengers annually pre-2020. A transformational expansion project — the Midfield Terminal Building (MTB) — opened in late 2023, adding a massive new terminal with a capacity of 45 million passengers per year, making AUH one of the largest airport terminals in the world by floor area.

Terminals

  • Midfield Terminal (Terminal A): Etihad Airways' primary hub terminal and new flagship building opened 2023. Handles the majority of Etihad flights and select partner airlines. Features 65 gates, premium lounges, extensive retail, and dining.
  • Terminal 1: Legacy international terminal, handling some international carriers and charter flights during the transition to the MTB.
  • Terminal 3: Dedicated Etihad Airways terminal (legacy), now supplementary to the MTB.
💡 Most Etihad flights now operate from the new Midfield Terminal (Terminal A). Check your boarding pass carefully for the correct terminal — free inter-terminal shuttle buses operate 24 hours. Allow at least 90 minutes for connections involving different terminals.

WiFi Availability

Free Wi-Fi is available throughout all AUH terminals under the network "AUH_FreeWifi" or via the Abu Dhabi Airports portal. Connect, register with an email or mobile number, and enjoy complimentary access with no advertised session time limit. High-speed premium Wi-Fi packages are available for purchase at lounge services desks for those requiring faster speeds for data-intensive tasks.

Transport to City Centre

Bus Services

The Abu Dhabi Department of Transport (DoT) operates bus routes from the airport to central Abu Dhabi. Route A1 connects the airport to the central bus station at Al Wahda Mall. Journey time is approximately 40–60 minutes. The fare is AED 4 (approx. USD 1.10). Buses run from 06:00 to 24:00 with 30-minute frequency.

Taxi and Rideshare

Abu Dhabi Taxi and Careem (which operates as the official taxi-booking platform in Abu Dhabi) are available from the taxi ranks outside arrivals. The journey to Abu Dhabi city centre takes 25–40 minutes depending on traffic and costs AED 70–120 (USD 19–33). Uber also operates in Abu Dhabi. Taxis to Dubai are available for approximately AED 250–350 (1.5–2 hours).

Airport Lounges

AUH boasts some of the finest airport lounges in the world, particularly the flagship Etihad lounges in the Midfield Terminal.

  • Etihad Airways The Residence Lounge (Terminal A): Ultra-exclusive lounge for The Residence (three-room suite) passengers with private butler service and restaurant-quality dining.
  • Etihad Airways First Class Lounge & Spa (Terminal A): World-renowned first class lounge with a la carte dining, full-service spa, showers, and sleep pods.
  • Etihad Airways Business Lounge (Terminal A): Large business class lounge with hot buffet, premium beverages, and dedicated work zones.
  • Plaza Premium Lounge (Terminal A): Accepts Priority Pass and DragonPass memberships; hot and cold dining, showers, and comfortable seating.
  • Al Dhabi Lounge (Legacy Terminals): Pay-per-use lounge in legacy terminals, available to all passengers.

Hotels Near the Airport

Several hotels are located on the airport island and in Abu Dhabi city, accessible within 30–40 minutes by taxi.

  • Rotana Hotels (On-site Airport): The Rotana Airport Hotel is directly connected to the terminal complex and ideal for transit passengers.
  • Yas Island Hotels: Yas Island (home to Ferrari World, Yas Marina Circuit) is approximately 10 km from the airport; options include the W Abu Dhabi Yas Island and Yas Hotel Abu Dhabi.
  • Emirates Palace Mandarin Oriental: Iconic ultra-luxury hotel approximately 35 km from the airport on the Abu Dhabi corniche.
  • The St. Regis Abu Dhabi: Luxury beachfront hotel approximately 30 km from the airport in the Nation Towers complex.
  • Marriott Hotel Al Forsan Abu Dhabi: Approximately 15 km from AUH in Khalifa City, convenient for the airport area.

Food and Restaurants

The Midfield Terminal (Terminal A) features an impressive array of dining options across its massive footprint.

  • The Chefs' Table (Terminal A): Multi-cuisine restaurant offering Arabic mezze, international dishes, and live cooking stations.
  • Starbucks (Terminal A and Terminal 3): Multiple outlets throughout the terminals, open around the clock during busy periods.
  • Le Petit Paris (Terminal A): French boulangerie offering croissants, baguette sandwiches, and coffee for early-morning travellers.
  • Shake Shack (Terminal A): American-style burgers and shakes airside, popular for quick meals.
  • Jones The Grocer (Terminal A): Australian specialty food retailer and café serving artisan coffee, fresh-baked goods, and gourmet sandwiches.
  • Qatar Airways Al Mourjan Café (airside): Light refreshments kiosk near airline gate areas for connecting passengers.

Travel Tips

  • US travellers: AUH is one of very few airports outside the US with pre-clearance facilities; US-bound passengers clear US Customs and Border Protection before departure, saving time on arrival in America.
  • Check which terminal your Etihad flight departs from — the Midfield Terminal (A) is now primary, but verify on your boarding pass.
  • The UAE dirham (AED) is pegged to the USD at approximately 3.67:1; currency exchange and ATMs are plentiful in all terminals.
  • Abu Dhabi has strict laws regarding public behaviour; dress modestly in the airport and respect local customs.
  • The Louvre Abu Dhabi, Grand Mosque (Sheikh Zayed), Yas Island, and Saadiyat Island are major attractions accessible within 30–45 minutes of the airport.
  • Dubai is approximately 90–120 minutes from AUH by taxi via the E11; many travellers visit both cities on a single trip.
  • Summer temperatures (May–September) regularly exceed 45°C outdoors; all airport areas are fully air-conditioned.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get from Abu Dhabi Airport to the city centre?

Bus Route A1 is the cheapest option at AED 4 and takes 40–60 minutes to reach central Abu Dhabi (Al Wahda area). Taxis and Careem cost AED 70–120 and take 25–40 minutes. Pre-booked hotel shuttles are also commonly available.

What is the new Midfield Terminal at Abu Dhabi Airport?

The Midfield Terminal (Terminal A) opened in late 2023 and is now the primary hub for Etihad Airways. It is one of the largest airport terminal buildings in the world by floor area, with 65 gates, a capacity of 45 million passengers per year, and premium facilities including world-class lounges, extensive retail, and diverse dining options.

Does Abu Dhabi Airport offer US pre-clearance for flights to America?

Yes, AUH is one of only a handful of airports outside the United States offering US Customs and Border Protection pre-clearance. Eligible passengers on US-bound Etihad flights clear immigration and customs at Abu Dhabi, arriving in the US as domestic passengers and avoiding immigration queues on arrival.

Which lounges at Abu Dhabi Airport accept Priority Pass?

The Plaza Premium Lounge in Terminal A accepts Priority Pass and DragonPass. Legacy terminals have the Al Dhabi pay-per-use lounge. Etihad's premium lounges (First, Business, and The Residence) are restricted to Etihad premium ticket holders and Etihad Guest/Oneworld status members.
