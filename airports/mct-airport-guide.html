Muscat International Airport Guide (MCT) – Muscat

IATA: MCT ICAO: OOMS Muscat, Oman 1 Terminal

Airport Overview

Muscat International Airport (MCT) is the primary gateway to the Sultanate of Oman, located approximately 28 km west of Muscat city centre. The airport opened its state-of-the-art new terminal in 2018, designed to handle 24 million passengers per year with architectural elements inspired by Omani heritage, including a striking wave-shaped roof referencing the Al Hajar mountain range. Oman Air serves as the national carrier and primary hub operator, and the airport handles an increasing number of low-cost carriers including Air Arabia Oman.

Terminals

  • Main Terminal (Single integrated terminal): All international and domestic flights operate from the unified 2018 terminal building. The terminal is divided into concourses A, B, and C, with Oman Air primarily using Concourse A and B, and other carriers assigned to C.
  • Royal Terminal: Dedicated VIP and state flights terminal separate from the main building.
💡 The main terminal at MCT is very large but logically designed. Airside transit between concourses is straightforward; follow colour-coded signage for your gate. The airport features beautiful Islamic geometric artwork throughout the interior.

WiFi Availability

Complimentary Wi-Fi is available throughout Muscat Airport under the network "MCT_Airport_WiFi". Connect and complete a simple registration via the landing page. There is no charge and no strict time limit. Connection speeds are generally very good, supporting video streaming and conference calls. SIM card purchase booths are also available near arrivals for travellers needing a local Omani number.

Transport to City Centre

Bus Services

Mwasalat public buses operate services from the airport to Muscat city centre (Ruwi bus station) and other key areas. The journey takes approximately 45–60 minutes. Fares are very affordable at OMR 0.5–1.0 (USD 1.30–2.60). Buses depart from the dedicated bus bays outside the arrivals exit and run throughout the day from early morning to late evening.

Taxi and Rideshare

Official airport taxis (white cabs) are available from the clearly marked taxi rank outside arrivals. There are no meters; negotiate or confirm the fare before departure. The journey to central Muscat (Ruwi/Muttrah) costs approximately OMR 6–12 (USD 15–31). Uber operates in Muscat and provides app-based transparent pricing; Careem is also available.

Car Rental

Major international car rental companies (Hertz, Avis, Budget, Enterprise, Europcar) and local Omani operators have desks in the arrivals hall. Oman's road infrastructure is excellent, and self-drive is highly recommended for exploring the country's dramatic landscapes.

Airport Lounges

Muscat Airport offers several premium lounge options for business class passengers and eligible cardholders.

  • Oman Air First Class Lounge (Concourse A): Exclusive first class lounge with a la carte dining, premium beverages, private showers, and spa treatment rooms.
  • Oman Air Business Class Lounge (Concourse A): Business lounge with hot buffet, beverages, and comfortable working spaces for business travellers.
  • Plaza Premium Lounge (Main Terminal): Accessible via Priority Pass, DragonPass, and pay-per-use. Full dining, showers, and relaxation facilities.
  • Marhaba Lounge (Concourse B): Pay-per-use lounge open to all passengers with light refreshments and fast Wi-Fi.

Hotels Near the Airport

Several hotels are conveniently located along the airport road and in key Muscat districts such as Seeb and Al Khuwair.

  • Crowne Plaza Muscat OCEC: Adjacent to the Oman Convention and Exhibition Centre, approximately 8 km from the airport, with modern facilities and multiple restaurants.
  • Radisson Blu Hotel Muscat: About 10 km from MCT in the Al Khuwair business district with pool and excellent dining.
  • Sheraton Oman Hotel: Approximately 20 km from the airport in Ruwi, a historic landmark hotel in the heart of old Muscat.
  • Ibis Muscat: Budget-friendly option approximately 12 km from the airport, clean and functional with a restaurant.
  • The Chedi Muscat: Luxury resort approximately 18 km from the airport near the Al Ghubrah beachfront, one of Oman's most acclaimed hotels.

Food and Restaurants

The modern terminal features a diverse selection of dining options spread throughout the departures area.

  • Tikka Nation (Concourse A): Indian-inspired restaurant serving biryanis, tikka dishes, and fresh breads; popular with South Asian travellers.
  • Starbucks (Main Terminal and Concourses): Multiple outlets providing coffee and light snacks throughout the terminal.
  • Burger King (Concourse B): Fast-food chain with full menu open during flight operations.
  • Costa Coffee (Concourse C): Coffee and pastries near the international departure gates.
  • Al Maha Restaurant (Arrivals hall): Traditional Omani cuisine including shuwa (slow-cooked lamb) and halwa dessert, great for a genuine local meal.
  • Dunkin' (Main Terminal): Donuts, sandwiches, and freshly brewed coffee for quick departures.

Travel Tips

  • Oman offers visa on arrival and eVisa options for most nationalities; verify your specific requirements on the Royal Oman Police immigration website before travel.
  • Oman is considered one of the safest and most welcoming countries in the Middle East; crime rates are very low for both residents and tourists.
  • The airport is approximately 28 km from central Muscat; plan transport in advance, especially for late-night arrivals when public buses are limited.
  • The Omani Rial (OMR) is subdivided into 1,000 baisa; currency exchange desks and ATMs are available in the arrivals hall.
  • Oman's summers (June–August) are extremely hot and humid; the best time to visit is October to March.
  • Muscat is spread across a very large geographic area; a rental car is strongly recommended for exploring the city and Oman's natural attractions.
  • The airport has dedicated family areas and prayer rooms throughout; service is generally efficient and courteous.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get from Muscat Airport to the city centre?

The Mwasalat public bus is the cheapest option at OMR 0.5–1.0, taking 45–60 minutes to Ruwi bus station. Official airport taxis cost OMR 6–12 to central Muscat and take about 30–40 minutes. Uber and Careem are available for app-based transparent pricing.

Is Wi-Fi free at Muscat Airport?

Yes, free Wi-Fi is available throughout the terminal under "MCT_Airport_WiFi." Registration via the landing page is required, but there is no charge or strict time limit. Connection quality is generally excellent in the modern 2018 terminal.

How large is the new Muscat Airport terminal?

The new terminal, which opened in 2018, covers approximately 580,000 square metres and is designed to handle up to 24 million passengers per year. It features three concourses (A, B, and C) and incorporates Omani architectural design elements throughout.

Does Muscat Airport have Priority Pass lounges?

Yes, the Plaza Premium Lounge accepts Priority Pass and DragonPass memberships. Pay-per-use access is available at the Marhaba Lounge. Oman Air's dedicated first and business class lounges are reserved for premium ticket holders and SkyTeam status members.
