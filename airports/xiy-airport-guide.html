Xi'an Xianyang International Airport Guide (XIY) – Xi'an

IATA: XIY ICAO: ZLXY Xi'an, China 3 Terminals

Airport Overview

Xi'an Xianyang International Airport (XIY) is the primary gateway to Xi'an, the ancient capital of China and home of the Terracotta Warriors, located approximately 47 km northwest of the city centre in Xianyang. The airport handled over 47 million passengers in 2019, making it one of China's busiest regional airports. Terminal 5 (T5), a massive new facility, opened in 2023 as part of a major expansion project, serving as the new primary hub for operations and capable of handling 83 million passengers annually at full capacity.

Terminals

  • Terminal 5 (T5): New primary terminal opened 2023; handles the majority of domestic and international flights including Air China, China Eastern, and Hainan Airlines international routes.
  • Terminal 3 (T3): Former primary international terminal, now handling overflow and some domestic carriers. Connected to T5 by automated transfer system.
  • Terminal 2 (T2): Original legacy terminal; reduced operations but still in use for some low-cost domestic flights.
💡 Verify your terminal carefully before heading to the airport — T5 is the new main hub, but some flights still use T3 or T2. Free inter-terminal shuttles are available, but connections require extra transit time.

WiFi Availability

Free Wi-Fi is available at XIY under the "XAAIRPORT_WIFI" network. Registration requires a Chinese mobile number for verification; international travellers without a Chinese SIM should look for open hotspot networks or purchase a local SIM card. As throughout China, social media and Western apps (Google, WhatsApp, Facebook) are blocked by the Great Firewall; a VPN configured before arrival is necessary to access these services.

Transport to City Centre

Airport Metro (Line 14)

Xi'an Metro Line 14 (Airport Line) connects T5 directly to Xi'an's city metro network. The line connects with Line 3 at Wenshang Road Station, providing access to the city centre in approximately 35–45 minutes total. Fares start at CNY 5. Trains operate from 06:00 to 23:00 with 8-minute peak frequency.

Bus Services

Airport express buses (Lines 1–6) serve key Xi'an destinations including the Bell Tower, High-Speed Railway Station, and the Tang Dynasty theme park district. Journey times range from 40–70 minutes. Fares are CNY 20–30. DiDi is also convenient and affordable.

Taxi and Rideshare

Taxis are available from the taxi rank outside each terminal's arrivals exit. The journey to Xi'an city centre (Bell Tower area) takes approximately 50–60 minutes and costs CNY 130–180 including tolls. DiDi provides app-based transparent pricing and is generally preferred by tech-savvy travellers.

Airport Lounges

XIY offers lounge facilities across its main terminals, with the best options in T5 and T3.

  • China Eastern Business Lounge (T5): Hot buffet including dim sum, noodles, and rice dishes; showers and business services available.
  • Hainan Airlines Business Lounge (T5): Premium lounge with curated Chinese cuisine and refreshments for Hainan Airlines business class passengers.
  • Air China VIP Lounge (T3): Star Alliance Gold accessible lounge with buffet dining and comfortable seating.
  • Plaza Premium Lounge (T5, International): Accepts Priority Pass and DragonPass; full hot dining and shower facilities.
  • DIVOtel Hotel Lounge (T5): Day-use hotel and lounge facilities for passengers seeking rest between long connections.

Hotels Near the Airport

Xi'an's airport area and surrounding Xianyang district offer various hotel options, with further choices in the city itself.

  • Xi'an Airport Langham Place (Xianyang): Luxury hotel approximately 3 km from T5 with extensive amenities and multiple dining venues.
  • Holiday Inn Xi'an Chanba: Approximately 20 km from the airport near the Chanba Ecological Zone, popular with business travellers.
  • Sofitel Xi'an on Renmin Square: Landmark luxury hotel in the city centre approximately 50 km from the airport.
  • Ibis Xi'an Xianyang Airport: Budget-friendly option approximately 2 km from the airport, offering shuttle service and no-frills rooms.
  • Hilton Xi'an High-Tech Zone: Modern business hotel approximately 40 km from the airport in the high-tech district.

Food and Restaurants

XIY's terminals feature a strong representation of Shaanxi cuisine alongside international options.

  • Lao Sun Jia (T5): Xi'an's famous chain serving biangbiang noodles and yangrou paomo (mutton bread stew) — essential local dining.
  • McDonald's (T5 and T3): Standard and China-exclusive items available; some locations open 24 hours.
  • Starbucks (T5): Two locations in the new terminal with the full menu.
  • Shaanxi Flavours Restaurant (T3): Traditional Xi'an snacks including roujiamo (Chinese hamburger) and cold skin noodles (liangpi).
  • KFC (T5): China-specific KFC menu with rice boxes, congee, and Sichuan wrap options alongside Western menu.
  • Luckin Coffee (T5): China's most popular domestic coffee chain, offering app-ordered delivery-style coffee at competitive prices.

Travel Tips

  • Xi'an is one of China's top tourist destinations with the Terracotta Warriors, ancient city walls, and the Muslim Quarter; allow at least 3 days to explore properly.
  • Install a VPN before arriving in China — Google Maps, WhatsApp, and most Western apps are blocked. Use Baidu Maps offline and Didi for rideshare.
  • China's 144-hour visa-free transit policy may apply to your nationality; check before booking and note that Xi'an (Shaanxi province) is included.
  • Mobile payment (WeChat Pay, Alipay) is essential; link a foreign Visa/MasterCard to Alipay before travel as many vendors don't accept cash.
  • T5 opened in 2023 and not all services may be fully ramped up; double-check restaurant and lounge opening hours for your specific concourse.
  • The airport to Xi'an drive can be long; the new metro is the most time-efficient option for most city-centre destinations.
  • Winter (December–February) in Xi'an can be cold with temperatures near freezing; pack accordingly.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get from Xi'an Airport to the city centre?

Metro Line 14 is the best option, connecting T5 to Xi'an's metro network in about 35–45 minutes for CNY 5. Airport express buses cost CNY 20–30 and take 40–70 minutes. Taxis and DiDi cost CNY 130–180 and take 50–60 minutes to the Bell Tower city centre area.

What is Terminal 5 at Xi'an Airport?

Terminal 5 (T5) opened in 2023 as Xi'an Airport's new primary terminal and the largest in northwestern China. It has a designed capacity of 83 million passengers annually and is now the main hub for domestic and international operations, replacing T3 as the primary terminal.

Can I visit the Terracotta Warriors on a layover in Xi'an?

China's 144-hour (6-day) visa-free transit policy is available for eligible nationalities including the Xi'an area. With a 10+ hour layover, a rushed Terracotta Warriors visit is possible (about 1 hour from the airport by taxi, 30-minute tour). For a proper visit, plan at least 3–4 hours at the site plus transport time.

Does Xi'an Airport have Priority Pass lounges?

Yes, the Plaza Premium Lounge in T5's international section accepts Priority Pass and DragonPass memberships. Airline lounges in T5 and T3 are available to business class passengers and SkyTeam/Star Alliance status holders depending on the carrier.
