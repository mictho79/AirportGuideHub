Charlotte Douglas International Airport Guide (CLT) – Charlotte

IATA: CLT ICAO: KCLT Charlotte, USA 1 Terminal

Airport Overview

Charlotte Douglas International Airport (CLT) is the primary hub for American Airlines and one of the ten busiest airports in the United States, serving approximately 60 million passengers per year. Located 7 miles west of downtown Charlotte, North Carolina, CLT punches far above its weight given the relatively modest size of the Charlotte metro area — its enormous passenger volume is largely driven by American Airlines using it as one of its two largest domestic hub-and-spoke operations. CLT offers nonstop service to over 170 destinations, including major European cities like London, Frankfurt, and Paris.

The airport operates as a single-terminal facility with five concourses (A, B, C, D, and E), all connected internally. This single-building design makes CLT one of the most connection-friendly airports in the country — the airport consistently ranks as one of the easiest for airline connections due to its compact, walkable layout and American Airlines' high concentration of departures here.

Terminals

CLT operates as a single main terminal building with five lettered concourses, all connected by internal walkways and moving sidewalks — no inter-terminal trains required.

  • Concourse A: American Airlines domestic and regional flights; Gates A1–A31
  • Concourse B: American Airlines domestic flights; Gates B1–B24
  • Concourse C: American Airlines domestic and international flights to Europe and Latin America; home to the Admirals Club and Flagship Lounge; Gates C1–C26
  • Concourse D: American Airlines domestic and some regional; Gates D1–D25
  • Concourse E: American Eagle/Envoy Air regional flights; Delta Air Lines, United Airlines, Southwest Airlines, Spirit, and other non-AA carriers; Gates E1–E37
💡 All concourses at CLT are connected internally — you can walk the entire airport without going through security again. Moving sidewalks connect the long connector corridors. Concourses A through D are American Airlines only; Concourse E handles all non-AA airlines.

WiFi Availability

Charlotte Douglas Airport provides complimentary WiFi throughout the terminal under the "CLT Airport WiFi" network. Connect by selecting the network and accepting the usage agreement — no time limits, passwords, or account registration required. Speed is generally adequate for email and browsing, though it can slow during peak travel periods. The airport has ongoing infrastructure upgrades to improve bandwidth capacity. USB charging stations are available at most gate areas throughout all concourses.

Transport to City Center

LYNX Blue Line Light Rail

The Charlotte Area Transit System (CATS) LYNX Blue Line provides rail service between CLT and uptown Charlotte. The airport station is located at the terminal's ground transportation level. The journey to Uptown Charlotte (CTC/Arena station) takes approximately 20 minutes and costs $2.20 per ride. Trains run approximately every 7–15 minutes during peak hours and every 20–30 minutes during off-peak times, from approximately 5:30 AM to 1:00 AM on weekdays.

Bus Services

CATS bus routes provide connections to various Charlotte neighborhoods. Several express shuttle companies serve the airport to surrounding suburban communities in Mecklenburg County and beyond. Hotel shuttles are widely available from the Ground Transportation Center.

Taxi and Rideshare

Taxis and rideshare services (Uber, Lyft) are available at the Ground Transportation Center on the lower level. A taxi to uptown Charlotte typically costs $25–$35 and takes 15–20 minutes. Rideshare fares run $15–$25. The light rail is the most reliable option during rush hour when I-85 can become congested.

Airport Lounges

As American Airlines' major hub, CLT has multiple excellent American lounges plus access points for other frequent flyers.

  • American Airlines Admirals Club – Concourse C: Primary Admirals Club with full food service, premium bar, and shower facilities; located near Gate C19
  • American Airlines Admirals Club – Concourse B: Second Admirals Club location for A/B gate travelers
  • American Airlines Flagship Lounge – Concourse C: Premium lounge exclusively for Flagship First and Flagship Business passengers; serves hot meals à la carte and offers shower suites
  • The Centurion Lounge – Concourse C: American Express Centurion Lounge with a focused menu and craft cocktail program
  • USO Lounge (Landside): Free lounge for active-duty military and their families

Hotels Near the Airport

Several hotels are located near CLT and offer complimentary shuttle service for travelers seeking convenient overnight accommodation.

  • Sheraton Charlotte Airport Hotel: Located directly across from the terminal with shuttle service; full-service amenities including a pool and restaurant
  • Hilton Charlotte Airport Hotel: Complimentary shuttle, 1 mile from the terminal, with a restaurant and fitness center
  • Embassy Suites Charlotte Airport: All-suite hotel with complimentary breakfast and evening reception, about 2 miles from the airport
  • Hyatt Place Charlotte Airport/Billy Graham Pkwy: Budget-friendly option with complimentary shuttle and continental breakfast
  • Marriott Charlotte Airport: Full-service Marriott with conference facilities and complimentary shuttle, approximately 3 miles from the terminal

Food and Restaurants

CLT has invested in local Carolina-inspired dining options alongside national chains throughout its five concourses.

  • Carolina Brew House (Concourse C): Local craft beers from the Carolinas alongside pub grub in a sports bar setting
  • Bojangles' Famous Chicken 'n Biscuits (Concourse B): The Charlotte-born Southern fried chicken chain — a quintessential Carolinas experience
  • Wendy's and Panda Express (Multiple concourses): Reliable quick-service options widely distributed across all five concourses
  • Cantina Laredo (Concourse D): Upscale Mexican restaurant with tableside guacamole and margaritas
  • Fox & Hound Pub (Concourse E): Sports bar with big screens, draft beers, and classic American pub food
  • Starbucks (Multiple locations): Available in Concourses A, B, C, and E

Travel Tips

  • CLT is known as one of the most efficient hub airports in the country — even 30-minute connections on American Airlines are routinely achievable thanks to the single-terminal design and compact layout.
  • Arrive 2 hours before domestic flights and 3 hours before international — security at CLT's main checkpoints can be lengthy, especially on Monday mornings and Friday evenings.
  • TSA PreCheck is available at multiple security lanes; CLEAR is also available at the main checkpoint.
  • The LYNX Blue Line from the airport is the most cost-effective transit option to uptown Charlotte — just $2.20 and about 20 minutes.
  • If connecting on American Airlines, the Flagship Lounge in Concourse C is open to Flagship Business passengers — a rare opportunity to use a premium lounge on a domestic connection flight.
  • Charlotte's weather can bring ice storms in winter — allow extra time and monitor flight status closely if traveling January through March.
  • The airport has an excellent public art program throughout all concourses featuring work by North and South Carolina artists.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get from Charlotte Douglas Airport to uptown Charlotte?

The LYNX Blue Line light rail is the most affordable and reliable option — approximately 20 minutes to uptown for $2.20. Taxis cost $25–$35 and rideshare (Uber/Lyft) runs $15–$25. Traffic on I-85 and Billy Graham Parkway can extend road travel times during rush hours.

Is WiFi free at Charlotte Douglas Airport?

Yes, CLT provides free WiFi throughout the terminal via the "CLT Airport WiFi" network. No password or registration is required, and there are no time limits. Speeds may slow during peak travel periods, but are generally adequate for browsing and email.

Can I walk between concourses at CLT without going through security again?

Yes — Charlotte Douglas is a single-terminal airport with all five concourses (A, B, C, D, E) connected by internal secure walkways and moving sidewalks. You can walk the entire length of the airport without exiting security or taking a train. This makes CLT one of the most connection-friendly airports in the U.S.

Does CLT have a Priority Pass lounge?

The Centurion Lounge in Concourse C is available to eligible American Express Platinum and Centurion cardholders. American Airlines Admirals Club locations do not accept Priority Pass. Check the Priority Pass app for current participating lounges at CLT, as offerings can change.
